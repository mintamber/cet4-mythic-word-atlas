# Vocabulary Example Source Policy

## Purpose

The examples in CET-4 Mythic Word Atlas are teaching material, not a live news feed or scholarly quotation database. Every word receives an original CET-4 example, a literary example, and a recent-world example.

## Attribution Types

- `original`: written for CET-4 learning; no external source claimed.
- `literary-adaptation`: an original sentence placed in the world, plot, or concerns of a named literary work. It is not the author's wording and is never displayed as a quotation.
- `public-domain-quote`: a short exact excerpt verified against a public-domain primary text. It must include title, author, and URL.
- `current-context`: an original sentence inspired by recognizable developments from recent years. It is not copied news prose and does not claim to be continuously updated.

## Inclusion Rules

- The target word or a natural inflected form must carry the intended meaning.
- English must be idiomatic, concise, and useful to an advanced CET-4 learner.
- Chinese translations must preserve meaning and register rather than mirror English word order.
- Recent-world examples may come from any still-relevant development in recent years; no single-year cutoff applies.
- Technology, politics, climate, science, culture, entertainment, business, education, sport, and China/global exchange should be represented without one topic dominating.
- Political and international examples use neutral language and avoid predictions, slogans, or partisan judgment.

## Exclusion Rules

- No fabricated quotations, titles, authors, publications, dates, or URLs.
- No news sentence is copied verbatim merely to sound authentic.
- No literary adaptation is placed inside quotation marks or described as an original line from the work.
- Rapidly changing numbers and office-holder claims are avoided unless necessary and verified.
- No celebrity gossip, private personal data, inflammatory framing, or unsupported accusation.

## Maintenance

When an example becomes misleading or stale, rewrite it as a broader recent-world context rather than merely changing a date. Exact quotations should be rechecked against the linked primary text before publication.
